  <name>Task 1: Derive linear stability condition for mean-field solution</name>
  <files>derivations/linear-stability-analysis.md</files>
  <action>
    Derive the linear stability of the mean-field free energy minimum.

    **Starting point from Phase 2:**
    Mean-field free energy density: f(m) = (J(q-1)/2q)m² - Ts(m)
    where s(m) is the entropy of the Potts model

    **Step 1: Self-consistency equation**
    The equilibrium order parameter satisfies ∂f/∂m = 0
    Linearize around the solution for small fluctuations δm

    **Step 2: Hessian matrix**
    Compute the second derivative: λ = ∂²f/∂m² evaluated at equilibrium
    This eigenvalue λ determines stability:
    - λ > 0: Stable minimum
    - λ < 0: Unstable maximum
    - λ = 0: Critical point

    **Step 3: Critical temperature from stability**
    Set λ(T_c) = 0 and solve for T_c
    Verify that this recovers the Phase 2 result: T_c = Jq/(q-1)

    **Step 4: Correlation length from linear response**
    Near T_c, the correlation length scales as ξ² ∼ 1/|T - T_c|
    This gives the mean-field exponent ν = 1/2

    **Step 5: Ginzburg criterion**
    Derive the Ginzburg criterion for mean-field validity:
    Gi ∼ (T_c - T)^{(4-d)/2}

    For d=2: Gi ∼ |T - T_c| (diverges at T_c, mean-field fails)
    For d=4: Gi ∼ constant (marginal case)
    For d>4: Gi → 0 (mean-field valid)

    Include ASSERT_CONVENTION at the top of the derivation file.
  </action>

    - [ ] Ginzburg criterion has correct dimension dependence (4-d)/2
