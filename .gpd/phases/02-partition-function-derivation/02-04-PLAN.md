  <name>Task 1: Derive mean-field critical temperature T_c</name>
  <files>.gpd/phases/02-partition-function-derivation/02-04-critical-points.md</files>
  <action>
Derive the critical temperature from mean-field free energy:

1. **Starting from mean-field free energy:**
   F = -NT ln[e^{βJ} + (q-1)]

2. **Order parameter expansion:**
   Near critical point, expand F in powers of order parameter m
   F(m) ≈ F_0 + a m² + b m^4 + ...

3. **Critical condition:**
   Phase transition occurs when m becomes non-zero
   This happens when coefficient of m² changes sign

4. **Derivation:**
   - Mean-field equation: m = tanh(βJ(q-1)m/q)
   - Critical point: βJ(q-1)/q = 1
   - Solution: T_c = Jq/(q-1)

5. **Check limits:**
   - q → 1 (Ising): T_c = 2J (correct mean-field Ising)
   - q → ∞: T_c → J (approaches coupling strength from above)

Write derivation with verification of limits.
  </action>
  <verify>
- [T_c] = [energy] (consistent with temperature units)
- q → 1 gives Ising T_c = 2J (standard mean-field result)
- T_c increases with q (harder to reach consensus with more diversity)
- Formula matches Wu (1982) reference
  </verify>

3. **Comparison table:**

   - In mean-field: phase transition occurs at T_c regardless of N
